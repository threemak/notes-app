## What is an Algorithm?

An **algorithm** is a step-by-step procedure or formula for solving a problem. It is a set of well-defined instructions designed to perform a specific task or solve a particular problem. Algorithms are the backbone of computer science and are used in various domains such as mathematics, data processing, artificial intelligence, and everyday life.
The word "algorithm" derives from the name of the Persian mathematician **Muhammad ibn Musa al-Khwarizmi** (780–850 AD). Al-Khwarizmi wrote a book called *"Al-Kitab al-Mukhtasar fi Hisab al-Jabr wal-Muqabala"* (*The Compendious Book on Calculation by Completion and Balancing*), which introduced systematic solutions to linear and quadratic equations. His name became synonymous with the term "algorithm" over time.

## How Algorithms Are Formed

Algorithms are typically formed through the following process:

1. **Problem Definition**: Clearly understand the problem you want to solve.
2. **Analysis**: Break the problem into smaller, manageable components.
3. **Design**: Create a step-by-step approach to solve each component.
4. **Implementation**: Write the algorithm in a language (human-readable or code).
5. **Testing**: Verify that the algorithm works as intended for all possible inputs.
6. **Optimization**: Refine the algorithm for efficiency and clarity.

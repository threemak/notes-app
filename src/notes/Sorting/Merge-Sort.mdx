Description is the mode for transmitting a mental image of the particulars of a story. 
Together with dialogue, narration, exposition, and summarization, 
it is one of the most widely recognized of the fiction-writing modes. 
As stated in Writing from A to Z, edited by Kirk Polking, 
it is more than the amassing of details; it is bringing something to life by carefully choosing and arranging words and phrases to produce the desired effect
